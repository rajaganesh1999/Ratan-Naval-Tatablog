About Ratan Naval Tata

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He was the chairman of Tata Group, from 1990 to 2012, and again, as interim chairman, from October 2016 through February 2017. He continues to head its charitable trusts.

Born into the prominent Tata family, he is the great-grandson of Jamsetji Tata, the founder of Tata Group. Ratan joined the company in 1961 after turning down a job with IBM offered by JRD Tata. He was initially assigned to Jamshedpur to work at Tata Steel. He worked on the shop floor of Tata Steel, shoveling limestone and handling the blast furnace.

Ratan Tata is notable for his business ethics, strategic vision, and commitment to innovation. His leadership transformed the Tata Group from a largely India-centric company to a global business, with operations in more than 100 countries across six continents.

Key Achievements

Global Expansion

Led Tata Group's international expansion with strategic acquisitions:

  • Tetley (2000) for $450 million
  • Corus Group (2007) for $12 billion
  • Jaguar Land Rover (2008) for $2.3 billion

These acquisitions transformed Tata from a largely India-centric company to a global conglomerate.

Business Growth

Under Ratan Tata's leadership, the group's revenues grew manifold:

  • 1991: $5.7 billion
  • 2011-12: Over $100 billion

This represented a compound annual growth rate of over 13% over two decades.

Innovation Focus

Established the Tata Innoverse program to foster innovation across the group. This led to the development of several groundbreaking products and services, including the Tata Swach water purifier and the Tata Ace mini-truck.

Philanthropic Efforts

Personal Contributions

Donated 65% of his shares in Tata Sons, valued at approximately $662 million, to charitable trusts.

Tata Trusts

As Chairman of Tata Trusts, oversees philanthropic activities that:

  • Support education initiatives
  • Provide healthcare services
  • Promote rural development
  • Advance scientific research

Disaster Relief

Led Tata Group's efforts in providing relief during natural disasters, including:

  • 2001 Gujarat earthquake
  • 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami
  • 2015 Nepal earthquake

Education Support

Donated $50 million to his alma mater, Cornell University, for a technology campus and $25 million to Harvard Business School for an executive center.
